Steps towards Financial Planning

In order to spur further growth of entrepreneurial initiative among women, leading private and public sector banks, have introduced various loan schemes that cater to this emerging segment.

BANK LOAN
ANNAPURNA SCHEME:

This loan is provided to women in the food catering industry, still establishing their small scale businesses. The loan allows these women entrepreneurs to avail it as capital requirements like buying equipment and utensils, setting up trucks, etc.

BANK LOAN
SHRINGAAR SCHEME:

Bhartiya Mahila Bank brings forth BMB Shringaar loan for self employed women or housewives for making a purchase of necessary equipments, establishing beauty parlors or meeting day-to-day expenses for running the business.

BANK LOAN
PARVARISH:

BMB Parvarish loan is extended for self employed women or housewives to establish a day-care centre.The loan can be availed for the purpose of the establishment of a child day care centre, purchase of utensils and other equipment.

GOVT. SCHEME
MUDRA YOJANA SCHEME FOR WOMEN:

This scheme was launched by the government of India to support enthusiastic women entrepreneurs who are seeking a business plan like starting a beauty parlour, tuition centre, a boutique etc. that makes them financially stronger with minimum efforts

BANK LOAN
TARUN LOAN:

For the well-established businesses which are planning to expand their reach and are short on capital, they can avail as much as INR 10 lacs for the purpose.Another feature of this scheme is that there is a Mudra card(credit card) which is also issued to the applicant of the loan which has a limit of 10% of the total loan amount sanctioned.

BANK LOAN
STREE SHAKTI PACKAGE:

Stree Shakti Scheme is an initiative by the State Bank of India (SBI). This scheme is uniquely tailored for the women who wish to become entrepreneurs or are wanting to expand their existing business.
